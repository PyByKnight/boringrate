Mississippi auto insurance rates are near the national average — but rising.

Mississippi drivers pay $2,438 per year on average. High uninsured motorist rates and an active litigation environment push premiums above what the state's rural profile might suggest.

BoringRate Editorial  ·  May 2026
TLDR
  • Mississippi averages around $2,438/year — above average relative to the state's income level
  • Rates rose +22% since 2023 — driven by litigation costs and severe weather exposure
  • Mississippi has one of the highest uninsured motorist rates in the country (~30% of drivers)
  • State Farm and USAA rank best; Mississippi has fewer carrier options than most states
  • The income-to-insurance ratio here makes shopping unusually impactful — even modest savings represent real dollars

Mississippi auto insurance averages $2,438 per year for full coverage — about 2% below the national average of roughly $2,500. For a largely rural state with lower-than-average traffic density, that near-average rate reflects two persistent cost pressures: one of the highest uninsured motorist rates in the country and a legal environment that produces costly personal injury litigation.

Mississippi consistently ranks among the top five states for uninsured drivers — estimates range from 23% to 29% of drivers carrying no insurance at all. That figure is staggering and has direct insurance implications. When an uninsured driver causes an accident, the injured party's own uninsured motorist coverage must pay. Carriers price that systemic risk into every policy sold in the state, and every insured driver subsidizes the uninsured ones through higher premiums.

The Jackson market and rural variation

Jackson and the surrounding Hinds County metro produce the highest premiums in the state, driven by higher accident frequency, elevated theft rates, and more litigation activity than rural Mississippi. A driver in Jackson can pay 40–60% more than a driver with the same profile in rural DeSoto or Rankin County. The Gulf Coast corridor — Gulfport, Biloxi, Hattiesburg — represents a middle ground, with rates reflecting both moderate traffic density and coastal weather exposure from hurricane and tropical storm risk.

Mississippi's low median income is relevant to the insurance market in one specific way: it contributes to the high uninsured rate. When insurance premiums represent a significant share of a household's budget, some drivers make the economically rational but illegal choice to drive uninsured. That drives up costs for everyone who does buy insurance. Shopping carriers at every renewal is the most effective tool for Mississippi drivers trying to keep their own costs in check in this environment.

Mississippi average: $2,438/year full coverage. National average: ~$2,500. Mississippi drivers pay about 2% less than the typical American driver — but high uninsured motorist rates mean strong UM/UIM coverage is especially important here.

Uninsured motorist coverage in Mississippi deserves special attention. Given the state's exceptionally high uninsured driver rate, buying the state minimum UM/UIM coverage is a meaningful risk. Increasing UM limits costs relatively little and provides substantial protection in a state where the odds of encountering an uninsured driver are among the highest in the nation.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cheapest car insurance in Mississippi?
State Farm and GEICO are the most competitive national carriers in Mississippi. Mississippi Farm Bureau is a state-exclusive member-based option that frequently prices below national carriers. USAA is the best option for military and veterans. Mississippi has moderate rates overall but very high uninsured motorist exposure — comprehensive coverage is important.

What are Mississippi's minimum auto insurance requirements?
Mississippi requires 25/50/25 liability coverage — $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident bodily injury and $25,000 property damage. Mississippi is a tort state with no mandatory PIP. Uninsured motorist coverage is offered but not mandatory. Given Mississippi's ~30% uninsured motorist rate (one of the highest in the US), uninsured motorist coverage is strongly recommended.

Why does Mississippi have such a high uninsured motorist rate?
Mississippi has the highest poverty rate of any US state, which means many drivers cannot afford insurance even when required. The uninsured motorist rate of approximately 30% means roughly 1-in-3 drivers you encounter may have no coverage. Uninsured motorist coverage on your own policy is the only real protection against an at-fault uninsured driver, and it is relatively inexpensive to add.

Is Mississippi Farm Bureau a good option?
Mississippi Farm Bureau is a strong regional option for residents who become Farm Bureau members (modest annual fee). It is particularly competitive for homeowners and rural residents and has consistently high satisfaction scores. Like most farm bureaus, it operates through agents rather than online — getting a quote requires a call or agent visit.

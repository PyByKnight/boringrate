Sacramento auto insurance rates in 2026 — what drivers here actually pay.

Sacramento averages ~$3,900/year for full coverage, +62% above the national average. Here’s what’s driving costs and which carriers compete hardest in this market.

BoringRate Editorial  ·  May 2026

Part of our California auto insurance report — statewide rates & carrier rankings.

TLDR
  • Sacramento averages ~$3,900/year for full coverage — +62% vs. the national average
  • GEICO and Wawanesa are consistently among the cheapest carriers in this market
  • Rate spreads are wide — cheapest carrier can be 40–60% below the most expensive for the same profile
  • California requires 30/60/15 liability (as of Jan 2025) — minimums leave significant gaps for most drivers
  • Rates are rising in 2026 — shopping at renewal is the single highest-ROI move most drivers can make

Sacramento drivers pay roughly $3,900/year for full coverage — +62% above the national average. Dense traffic, higher repair labor rates, elevated theft risk, and complex claims all factor into metro pricing.

Within California, the Sacramento metro runs differently from rural parts of the state. Urban density increases accident probability, and theft and vandalism rates are higher in most metro cores. Carriers price these risks into their Sacramento rates regardless of an individual driver’s record.

How to find the cheapest carrier in Sacramento

The single most important thing a Sacramento driver can do is compare rates at every renewal. Carriers update their pricing models frequently, and the cheapest option last year may not be cheapest today. The spread between the highest and lowest rate for the same driver profile is typically 40–60% in this market — that’s hundreds of dollars per year on the table.

California coverage rule: California requires 30/60/15 liability (as of Jan 2025). These are legal minimums — full coverage (comprehensive + collision) provides substantially better protection for most Sacramento drivers.

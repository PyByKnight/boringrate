Country Financial Auto Insurance Review 2026

Country Financial is the Midwest's most comprehensive insurance and financial services carrier — available in 19 states with competitive auto pricing and exclusive agents who manage your insurance alongside your retirement and investment accounts.

BoringRate Editorial  ·  May 2026
TLDR
  • Prices approximately 14% below Midwest region state averages
  • Available in 19 states, primarily Midwest — not nationwide, but broader than most pure regional carriers
  • Full-service model: insurance, retirement planning, and investments through one exclusive agent relationship
  • Exclusive captive agent model — all policies written and serviced through Country Financial agents
  • NAIC complaint ratio 0.81 — slightly below industry average; solid service record across diverse Midwest geography

Country Financial (formerly Country Companies) was founded in 1925 in Bloomington, Illinois as an agricultural insurance provider and has evolved into one of the Midwest's most broadly available full-service financial services companies. It operates in 19 states — primarily across the Midwest and parts of the South and West — through exclusive captive agents who offer not just auto and home insurance but also retirement accounts, annuities, and investment products. This breadth makes Country Financial more comparable to Northwestern Mutual or New York Life in its agent model than to a pure regional auto carrier.

Country Financial prices approximately 14% below Midwest state averages across standard driver profiles. The pricing advantage is most pronounced in its core Illinois, Minnesota, and Iowa markets. In states where it competes with specialized regional carriers like Indiana Farm Bureau or Shelter Insurance, the pricing differential is narrower — those carriers' deeper state concentration often gives them a slight pricing edge.

The full-service advantage

Country Financial's distinctive positioning is its combination of insurance and financial services. A Country Financial agent manages your auto and home policies alongside your retirement accounts and investment portfolio. For Midwest households that prefer a single trusted advisor for all financial decisions — rather than a separate insurance agent and a separate financial advisor — this model provides a meaningful convenience and relationship depth. Your agent's knowledge of your full financial picture (assets, dependents, income trajectory) can inform insurance recommendations that a pure-insurance agent wouldn't make.

This approach also means Country Financial clients tend to have high retention rates — when your retirement account is with the same company as your auto policy, switching insurers at renewal requires more friction. Country Financial's renewal pricing reflects this dynamic: its renewal factors are more stable than pure-price-competition carriers like Progressive, but it doesn't offer the aggressive loyalty discounts of State Farm or USAA.

Who it's right for

Country Financial is strongest for Midwest homeowners who want to consolidate their financial and insurance relationships. Its bundling discounts for auto + home are competitive, and adding life insurance or a retirement account can produce additional multi-policy benefits. For drivers who want only auto insurance and the lowest possible price, a more focused regional carrier (Shelter in Central states, Indiana Farm Bureau in Indiana, Iowa Farm Bureau in Iowa) may have a slight pricing edge.

Watch-outs

Country Financial's 19-state footprint excludes significant markets (California, the Northeast, Florida, much of the South). Its complaint ratio — while below average at 0.81 — trails the best regional specialists. The full-service model means your agent may spend as much time on financial planning as on insurance optimization. Drivers who prioritize price-only comparison will find that Country Financial's pricing strength is real but not the most aggressive available in most of its markets.

Bottom line: Country Financial is the right carrier for Midwest households seeking a full-service financial relationship — not just the cheapest auto insurance. If you value consolidating insurance, retirement accounts, and financial planning through one agent, Country Financial's model is genuinely differentiated. For pure price comparison, benchmark it against Shelter Insurance and your state's Farm Bureau carrier before deciding.

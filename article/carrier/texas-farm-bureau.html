Texas Farm Bureau Auto Insurance Review 2026

J.D. Power #1 in Texas for 11 consecutive years, with prices routinely 25% below the state average. If you live in Texas and haven't gotten a Farm Bureau quote, you're probably overpaying.

BoringRate Editorial  ·  May 2026
TLDR
  • Prices roughly 25% below Texas's state average — often the cheapest option available for TX residents
  • J.D. Power #1 in Texas, 11 consecutive years — the most sustained satisfaction record of any carrier in the state
  • Membership required (~$35–40/yr for basic TFB membership) — a small cost relative to premium savings
  • Texas-only carrier — county-level agent offices across the state, no online quoting
  • Particularly strong for rural and suburban households; TFB agents know local risk profiles in depth

Texas Farm Bureau Insurance has been insuring Texas drivers since 1952 and operates through a network of county-level agents across all 254 Texas counties. It is the only carrier in the state to have earned J.D. Power's #1 ranking for customer satisfaction in Texas for 11 consecutive years — a record that reflects both competitive pricing and a claims experience that consistently outperforms national carriers.

On pricing, TFB operates approximately 25% below the state average across standard driver profiles. With Texas's state average around $3,136 per year, that translates to effective premiums closer to $2,350 for a clean-record driver. Add back the $35–40 annual membership and the math still runs substantially in TFB's favor against GEICO, State Farm, or Allstate.

J.D. Power — 11 years at #1

J.D. Power measures satisfaction on claims handling, pricing, billing, and agent interaction. TFB's streak at #1 in the Texas region is not a marketing claim — it's verified third-party data. What makes it notable is that most carriers cycle in and out of top positions as they adjust pricing and staffing. TFB's consistency across 11 years suggests the underlying model — local county agents who know their community — produces genuinely better outcomes, particularly at claim time.

The county agent structure means your agent likely lives near you, understands local weather patterns and road conditions, and has managed claims for neighbors. This differs meaningfully from a national carrier's call center, where your claim may be handled by someone unfamiliar with Tornado Alley hail patterns or Houston flooding cycles.

The membership requirement

To purchase TFB insurance, you must hold a Texas Farm Bureau membership, which costs approximately $35–40 per year for the basic level. Membership is open to any Texas resident — you do not need to be a farmer or operate agricultural land. The membership fee is effectively a small subscription to access what is often the cheapest insurer in the state. Most TFB policyholders recover the membership cost within the first month of premium savings.

Agent-only model

Texas Farm Bureau does not offer online quotes or policy management. All interactions — initial quote, policy changes, claims — go through your county agent. This is a real limitation for drivers accustomed to GEICO's or Progressive's fully digital experience. But it is also precisely why TFB's service scores are high: when you file a claim, you are calling a person who knows your household, not navigating an automated system.

Watch-outs

TFB covers Texas residents only. If you relocate out of state, you will need a new carrier. The agent-only model means no instant quotes and no self-service app. Some TFB agents have long wait times during high-claim periods (major hail events, flooding). For drivers who need SR-22 filing or have non-standard profiles, TFB may decline coverage — it primarily insures standard risk profiles.

Complaint record

Texas Farm Bureau's NAIC complaint ratio runs consistently below 0.70 — meaning it receives roughly 30% fewer complaints per premium dollar than the national average. For a carrier of its volume and market share in Texas, this is a strong outcome and reinforces what J.D. Power measures: the claims experience is efficient and the disputes are rare.

Bottom line: If you are a Texas resident with a standard driving profile, Texas Farm Bureau should be the first call on your comparison list. The combination of 25% below-average pricing, J.D. Power #1 for 11 years, and a local agent who knows Texas risk is hard to match. The trade-offs — membership requirement, no online quotes, TX-only — are real but minor relative to what you get. Get a quote from your county agent before committing to any national carrier.
